Zone 1 & 2: Administrative Machinery & Leadership

Section 4: Municipal Authorities Charged with Execution 4 = The Four Pillars of Command

Smart Memory: A giant digital billboard at the lobby entrance listing the supreme administrative pillars of the city.

Exam Details: Explicitly charges four authorities with executing this Act: (a) The Corporation, (b) The Standing Committee, (c) The Municipal Commissioner, and (where applicable) (d) The Transport Committee.

Question Hook: Paper setters love to ask which body is not charged with execution under Section 4. The Mayor is not an executing authority; the Mayor is a political/presiding head.
Section 5: Constitution of Corporation 5 = Birth Certificate of the Corporation

Smart Memory: A baby being born inside the main assembly hall and instantly handed an official corporation seal.

Exam Details: Officially defines the Corporation as a Body Corporate. Statutory keywords include Perpetual Succession, Common Seal, power to hold property, and the capacity to sue and be sued under its corporate title.

Section 6: Duration of Corporation 6 = The 5-Year Lifespan Clock

Smart Memory: A clock on the wall ticking down exactly 60 months (5 years).

Exam Details: Sets the definitive term of the Corporation at 5 years from the date appointed for its first meeting, unless dissolved earlier.

Section 7: Resignation of Office by Councillors 7 = Stepping Down

Smart Memory: An elected member walking out of the building throwing down a resignation letter.

Exam Details: Governs the process by which elected representatives resign. A Councillor submits a signed written resignation directly to the Mayor.

! Syllabus Trap

Students mistake Section 7 for composition. Composition parameters (Wards and Elections) span from Sections 5 to 18 under the sub-headings Qualifications and Disqualifications and Election of Councillors. Wards are the units from which Corporators are elected.

Section 10: Disqualification for being a Councillor 10 = Show Me the Exit / Disqualification Card

Smart Memory: A candidate being kicked out of the building for hiding an illegal corporate contract.

Exam Details: Lists the strict bars on holding a seat. High-yield criteria include: Holding an office of profit under the Corporation, failing to pay municipal dues within a specified timeline after receiving an official notice, being an undischarged insolvent, or having a direct/indirect financial interest in any corporate contract or employment.

Section 19: Mayor and Deputy Mayor 19 = Mayor on the Throne

Smart Memory: Gate No. 19 leads to a grand throne room where the First Citizen (the Mayor) welcomes the public.

Exam Details: Commands the Corporation to choose a Mayor and a Deputy Mayor from among its elected Councillors. The Mayor acts as the constitutional head and presides over General Body meetings.

! The Exam Trap

The Mayor holds deliberative and political supremacy, not executive authority. Do not confuse political oversight with executive command.

Section 20: Constitution of Standing Committee 20 = Money Guardians

Smart Memory: A high-security bank vault door guarded by 20 strict financial accountants checking budget spreadsheets.

Exam Details: This is the most powerful internal committee within the Corporation's infrastructure. It acts as the financial screening engine, exercising structural control over corporate expenses, scrutinizing budget applications, and auditing corporate tenders before general board evaluation.

Section 25: Appointment of Transport Committee 25 = The Transport Desk

Smart Memory: A miniature model of a city bus numbered 25 sitting on a committee table.

Exam Details: Regulates the composition of the committee tasked with managing, structuring, and scaling the public transport undertaking across the city.

Section 35: Proceedings Valid Until Contrary Proved 35 = Shield of Legitimacy

Smart Memory: A judge slamming a gavel marked 35 to protect municipal files from a minor technical typo lawsuit.

Exam Details: Protects the legitimacy of municipal governance. It states that even if an error, vacancy, or later disqualification of a member is discovered, all decisions and transactions made during meetings remain perfectly valid unless clear proof of bad faith or systemic invalidity is established in court.

Zone 3 & 4: Executive Staff & Essential Services

Section 36: Appointment of the Commissioner 36 = CEO's Office

Smart Memory: Room No. 36 has a huge gold plaque reading: "CHIEF EXECUTIVE OFFICER (CEO)".

Exam Details: The absolute administrative spine of the Act. The Municipal Commissioner is appointed by the State Government and exercises supreme executive power over daily operations. Remember that Section 36 defines who the executive head is, while Section 63 defines what the corporation provides for.

Sections 45 & 46: Statutory Officers & Duties 45/46 = The City Brains

Smart Memory: An engineer holding blueprints at desk 45 handing them to a Medical Officer with a stethoscope at desk 46.

Sec. 45: Governs the appointment of essential statutory posts: City Engineer, Medical Officer of Health (MOH), Municipal Chief Auditor, and Municipal Secretary.

Sec. 46: Defines the powers and duties of the City Engineer and the MOH.

Food Safety Context: The MOH is the primary authority coordinating local health, disease vectors, and contamination sanitation containment.

Section 47: Powers and Duties of Municipal Chief Auditor 47 = The Account Scanner

Smart Memory: An auditor at desk 47 wielding a giant red pen to double-check municipal ledger sheets.

Exam Details: Empowers the Chief Auditor to run independent, continuous audits of corporate expenditure. The Auditor reports directly to the Standing Committee to prevent financial anomalies.

Section 61: Essential Services Restrictions 61 = Essential Services No-Exit Rule

Smart Memory: Emergency workers locked to their desks by chain 61 during a massive city storm.

Exam Details: Blocks municipal emergency staff from abandoning their duties. Employees classified under essential operations (such as conservancy, public health, fire response, and waterworks engineers) cannot resign or stop work without written authorization, ensuring city services do not collapse.

Section 62: State Power to Declare Emergency 62 = The State Emergency Button

Smart Memory: The Governor activating an emergency siren shaped like a 62 to override a city strike.

Exam Details: Gives the State Government authority to step in. If public health, safety, or basic infrastructure is threatened by strikes or major disruptions, the State can issue an emergency declaration to secure, restore, or take control of municipal operations.

Zone 5, 6 & 7: Core Mandates, Assets & Treasury

Section 63: Obligatory Duties of the Corporation (★ Super Critical) 63 = The Non-Negotiable Checklist

Smart Memory: A massive legal document containing 63 checkboxes that the Corporation must tick off every day to keep the city alive.

Exam Details: The single most tested section for municipal functions. It lists the items the Corporation must fulfill using its resources:

  • Clause (2): Scavenging, street watering, and cleansing of public walkways.
  • Clause (3): The collection, removal, treatment, and safe disposal of sewage, offensive waste, and Solid Waste Management.
  • Clause (4): Building, maintaining, and clearing public drains, water-closets, and urinals.
  • Clause (6): Constructing and running public hospitals, including specialized facilities for isolating contagious diseases.
  • Clause (11): Securing or dismantling dangerous structures.
  • Clause (12): Registering births and deaths.
  • Clause (17): Establishing and regulating public markets and slaughterhouses.
  • Clause (20): Managing municipal waterworks to secure a constant supply of safe, wholesome drinking water.
Section 66: Discretionary Duties 66 = The Optional/Discretionary Checklist

Smart Memory: A luxury lounge numbered 66 featuring designs for public parks, museums, and transport options.

Exam Details: Covers matters the Corporation may choose to fund if the budget permits (e.g., establishing public gardens, building public theaters, setting up relief stalls during scarcity, or organizing educational exhibitions).

Section 67: Executive Functions of Authorities 67 = Executive Vested Power

Smart Memory: The Commissioner standing in hall 67 pointing to an emblem reading "Supreme Executive Execution Power".

Exam Details: Explicitly maps out where executive authority rests. It clearly states that the overall executive power for carrying out the provisions of this Act vests directly in the Municipal Commissioner, subject to approval limits set by the Corporation or Standing Committee.

Section 68: Power Under Other External Laws 68 = The External Enforcement Link

Smart Memory: The Commissioner wielding an extra set of keys labeled 68 to unlock national health laws.

Exam Details: Broadens the enforcement reach of the executive head. It allows the Municipal Commissioner to seamlessly exercise powers and enforcement mechanisms granted under separate state or central acts (such as national food safety protocols, environmental protection acts, or public health declarations) using the corporate machinery.

Section 76: Acquisition of Property 76 = Property Acquisition

Smart Memory: A legal deed stamped 76 allowing AMC to buy land for a public food testing laboratory.

Exam Details: Empowers the Corporation to buy, receive, lease, or hold property. Gives the municipality the legal power to acquire both movable and immovable assets inside or outside city borders for the purposes of the Act (such as building public testing labs, cold storage sites, or new municipal markets).

Section 79: Disposal of Municipal Property 79 = Property Disposal Lock

Smart Memory: A barrier gate labeled 79 stopping the unapproved sale of municipal market land.

Exam Details: Restricts how city land and assets can be sold, leased, or transferred. The Commissioner cannot sell or lease municipal property for long periods without the formal sanction and approval of the Standing Committee or the Corporation.

Section 82: Constitution of Municipal Fund 82 = The Master Money Vault

Smart Memory: A giant titanium cash locker labeled 82 where every stream of public tax revenue accumulates.

Exam Details: Formally sets up the central treasury account. All money received by or on behalf of the Corporation—including civic taxes, registration fees, licensing fines, and state grants—is pooled into a single account known as the Municipal Fund.

Section 83: Lodgment of Money Collections 83 = Money Lodgment Duty

Smart Memory: The Commissioner hauling bags of revenue straight to a bank teller window numbered 83.

Exam Details: Designates the legal handler of all incoming corporate revenues. The Commissioner is personally responsible for receiving all incoming city moneys and must promptly lodge them into approved banks.

Section 88: Lawful Spending Limits 88 = Lawful Spending Bounds

Smart Memory: A strict rules booklet marked 88 defining exactly what the city cash can be legally spent on.

Exam Details: Defines the lawful spending limits of city funds. Money within the Municipal Fund can only be spent on tasks expressly authorized by the GPMC Act or other laws governing municipal operations.

Section 90: Urgent Emergency Spending Payments 90 = Emergency Spending Escape Valve

Smart Memory: An emergency axe breaking glass box 90 to pull out urgent cash to patch a broken water main.

Exam Details: Provides a way to bypass standard budget rules during unexpected public emergencies. If a sudden disaster or public crisis threatens city health or infrastructure (such as an outbreak or a contaminated water system), the Commissioner can authorize immediate spending for urgent works, even if the funds aren't covered by a current budget grant. It requires prompt notification to the Standing Committee.

Section 93: Accounting Prescriptions 93 = Standing Committee Ledger Rules

Smart Memory: A strict double-entry ledger book marked 93 detailing corporate tracking codes.

Exam Details: Mandates accounting standards. The layout, ledgers, and tracking format of municipal accounts must be maintained according to the specific formatting rules set by the Standing Committee.

Zone 8, 9 & 10: Special Billing, Engineering & FSO Core Weapons

Section 127: Taxes Imposed under this Act 127 = The Tax Catalog

Smart Memory: A high-speed sorting machine filing invoices into 127 different tax categories.

Exam Details: Lists the revenue engines that fund the city's public health works. Core taxes include: (1) Property Taxes (comprising general tax, water tax, conservancy tax, and drainage tax), (2) Tax on Vehicles, Boats, and Animals, (3) Theatre Tax, and (4) Mobile Tower Taxes.

Section 128: Manner of Recovering Taxes 128 = Tax Recovery Methods

Smart Memory: A collection officer issuing a strict property attachment warrant marked 128.

Exam Details: Sets out the legal steps for collecting unpaid taxes. Enforcement actions include serving an official bill, issuing a demand notice, attaching movable or immovable property, or initiating legal recovery lawsuits.

Section 134: Water Supply Charges by Measurement / Compounding 134 = Volume Billing / Industry Water Meter

Smart Memory: A high-tech digital water meter calculating consumption for a large sweetmeat factory, marked with a 134 stamp.

Context & Details: High-volume operations like food processing plants, commercial kitchens, sweetmeat factories, and slaughterhouses consume water at a level that doesn't align with basic property values. Section 134 provides the alternative system:

1. Clause (a) - Charging by Measurement: The Commissioner can charge for water by a meter or measurement at specific rates set by the Standing Committee.

2. Clause (b) - Compounding: The Commissioner can sign an agreement with a business owner for a renewable term of 1 to 5 years, charging a fixed periodical sum instead of strict meter readings.

FSO Exam Hook: Watch out for questions on the maximum duration allowed when compounding for a water supply under Section 134. It cannot exceed 5 years at one time.
Section 153: Construction of Drains 153 = The Sewage Highway

Smart Memory: A deep underground drainage pipeline layout stretching for 153 kilometers.

Exam Details: Gives the executive branch full authority over the city's sewage network. Poor drainage leads directly to water contamination and pest vectors. Section 153 gives the Commissioner power to lay out, construct, expand, or fix sewers across public or private land.

Section 189: Provision of Corporate Water Supply 189 = Potable Water Delivery Protection

Smart Memory: A giant clean water reservoir numbered 189 supplying drinking water across the city map.

Exam Details: Mandates the engineering and protection of municipal water networks to ensure a reliable supply of safe, pure drinking water.

Section 194: Vesting of Water Works 194 = Source Water Ownership

Smart Memory: An official copper shield mounted on a municipal pump station reading "Property of AMC - Code 194".

Exam Details: Establishes ownership of water infrastructure. It states that all public water reservoirs, mains, pumps, conduits, and engineering components vest entirely in the Corporation, creating the legal chain of custody for water safety before it reaches consumers.

Section 199: Pushing Water Pipes through Private Land 199 = Pipeline Infrastructure Push

Smart Memory: A drilling rig pushing a clean water pipe right underneath fence line 199.

Exam Details: Grants the Commissioner structural authority to lay water mains, pipes, or conduits through, under, or across any private land or street after giving a reasonable written notice. This ensures grid extensions to food processing zones or public markets cannot be blocked by private land disputes.

Section 202: Vesting of Public Streets 202 = Public Highway Ownership

Smart Memory: Standing on a wide road marked "Route 202" with an official sign reading: "Property of the Corporation."

Exam Details: Declares all public roads, pavements, sidewalks, and structural materials property of the Corporation, placing them under the direct control of the Commissioner.

Section 210: Power to Prescribe Street Lines 210 = Road Boundaries

Smart Memory: A yellow laser marking a perfect boundary line down boulevard 210.

Exam Details: Regulates road boundaries to prevent layout issues. Encroachments and immediate clearance of items hawked or deposited on public walkways without notice are governed further down under Section 231.

Section 253 & 254: Notice to Erect Buildings 253 = Prior Building Plan Approval

Smart Memory: A builder carrying blueprints marked 253 waiting in line for formal structural authorization.

Exam Details: Stops unauthorized construction. Anyone planning to build or structurally modify a property must first file a formal notice accompanied by certified architectural blueprints.

Section 264: Unsafe or Dangerous Buildings 264 = Dangerous Structure Demolition

Smart Memory: A building shaking structurally with a big red warning sign painted across it: "DEMOLISH OR REPAIR - ORDER 264".

Exam Details: Public safety protection power. If an ongoing inspection shows a building is structurally unstable, dilapidated, or dangerous to human life, the Commissioner can order the owner to secure, vacate, or demolish it.

Section 290 & 291: Scavenging and Waste Ownership 290/291 = Waste Management Rules

Sec. 290 (The Clean Street Sweep): Mandates regular street cleaning and waste management. The Commissioner must ensure public streets are swept and all urban refuse and filth is systematically removed.

Sec. 291 (Waste Ownership Law): All collected municipal waste, sweepings, sewage, and commercial garbage legally belong to the Corporation, allowing for centralized disposal or recycling processing.

Section 296 & 297: Sanitary Entries & Whitewashing Orders 296/297 = Sanitary Clean Check

Sec. 296 (Sanitary Entry Power): Gives the executive wing authority to enter and inspect properties to verify sanitary compliance and identify health hazards.

Sec. 297 (Mandatory Whitewashing Order): If an industrial site or building is unhygienic, the Commissioner can issue a notice requiring the owner to whitewash, clean, or disinfect the premises.

Section 315 & 318: Disease Containment & Closure of Eating Houses 315/318 = Outbreak Controls

Sec. 315 (Unannounced Disease Containment Protocol): Emergency containment authority during infectious or waterborne outbreaks. Authorizes unannounced entries to disinfect or isolate vectors.

Sec. 318 (Shut Food Outlets): Empowers health officers to shut down eating places, food outlets, or water stations spreading infectious diseases.

Section 337 & 338: Unwholesome Food & Search/Seizure Tools 337/338 = Core Enforcement Weapons

Sec. 337 (Banning Decayed Food): An absolute must-know baseline for food inspectors. This section explicitly bans anyone from selling, keeping, or exposing for sale any unwholesome food, diseased meat, or adulterated ingredients within an official municipal market.

Sec. 338 (The FSO's Search & Seizure Tool): Grants the authority to inspect live animals, raw ingredients, and foods intended for human consumption. Crucially, it extends to the full inspection of vessels, pots, utensils, and manufacturing tools used in food preparation. Gives inspectors statutory authority to seize any item found to be unwholesome, adulterated, or diseased on the spot.

Section 376 & 376A: Trade Licensing & Instant Police Shutdowns 376/376A = Gatekeeper Protocols

Sec. 376 (Gatekeeper License): Bars commercial kitchens, food manufacturers, and industrial trades from operating without a formal corporate health license.

Sec. 376A (Instant Closure / Police Force): If a trade is caught operating without a valid license, or is violating safety conditions to a degree that endangers public health, the Commissioner can take immediate steps to halt the use of the premises, including using police assistance to seal the doors.

Section 380, 381 & 383: Street Sales & Slaughter Control 380/381/383 = Meat & Street Food Supervision

Sec. 380 (No Open Slaughter): Strictly bans the slaughter of cattle, sheep, goats, or pigs for human consumption at any place outside a registered municipal or licensed private slaughterhouse.

Sec. 381 (Sugarcane & Cut Fruit Rule): Regulates street vendors selling items like fresh juices, aerated water, mobile ice-golas, kulfi, or cut/peeled fruits outside authorized municipal markets.

Sec. 383 (Confiscating Unlicensed Carts): Authorizes the immediate seizure and confiscation of mobile vendor carts operating unhygienically or without structural alignment to licensing permissions.

Section 472 & 481: Continuing Fines & Court Prosecutions 472/481 = Courtroom Enforcement

Sec. 472 (Daily Recurring Fine): Imposes escalating daily compounding fines on an establishment that continues to operate in an unhygienic manner after receiving an official health notice.

Sec. 481 (Launch Court Prosecutions): Empowers the Municipal Commissioner to launch, manage, or withdraw civil and criminal prosecutions against any individual violating licensing terms, sanitation rules, or public health guidelines.

🎯 Top Exam Tips for the Food Safety Officer (FSO) Exam

  • 1. The Vessels & Pots Trap: If an exam question specifically highlights the authority to inspect vessels, pots, or manufacturing instruments used to prepare food, point directly to Section 338, not standard licensing rules.
  • 2. The Street Food Composition: If a question describes an unlicensed cart hawking mobile operational items like ice-golas, aerated water, kulfi, sugarcane juice, or cut/peeled fruits, select Section 381.
  • 3. The Daily Penalty Compounding Logic: If a food facility fails to clean its dirty building infrastructure after receiving a notice under Section 297, remember that Section 472 allows for an escalating daily compounding fine on top of the initial ticket penalty under Section 471.
